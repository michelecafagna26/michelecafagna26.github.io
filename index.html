Michele Cafagna

Marie Curie PhD Fellow and Machine Learning Researcher

I'm currently a Marie Curie PhD Fellow at the Institute of Linguistics & Language Technology at the University of Malta supervised by Prof. Albert Gatt and co-supervised by Prof. Kees van Deemter. I'm also an Early Stage Researcher in the NL4XAI Project, which is part of the project Horizon2020 funded by the EU. Previously, I worked as a Machine Learning Research Scientist at Aptus.AI, a RegTech startup based in Pisa, Italy, I've been Visiting Researcher at the Center for Language and Cognition of the University of Groeningen (CLCG) in the Netherlands where I worked on my Master Thesis project on Natural Language Generation, I've also collaborated with the ItaliaNLP Lab at the Institute of Linguistics of the National Research Center (ILC-CNR) based in Pisa.

Research Interests

My current research focuses on Vision & Language Deep Learning models and Multimodal Grounding and Explainability. But I'm also interested in Natural Language Processing and Generation, Computer Vision and Deep Multimodal Learning.
